About This Object

This object shows a classroom setting where students are interacting with technology. I chose it because it connects to the history of educational technology and helps show how classrooms have changed over time.

Historical Context

This photograph comes from the United Press International (UPI) photo collection archived through Chapman University Digital Commons and shared through the Digital Public Library of America. The image shows students using computers in a classroom, reflecting an important period when schools were beginning to introduce digital technology into learning environments.

The object highlights how educational institutions gradually adopted technology to prepare students for a more digital future. While this image predates modern artificial intelligence tools such as ChatGPT and adaptive learning systems, it shows an earlier stage of technology integration in classrooms.

I like to compare artificial intelligence to tools like calculators. Before students are allowed to rely on calculators for mathematical equations, they are first expected to understand foundational skills such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Using calculators too early can prevent students from understanding the reasoning behind solving equations. I believe AI functions in a similar way. Students should first understand important concepts before relying heavily on AI tools.

For example, if a student asks AI to build an entire platform or complete a project without understanding the process, troubleshooting and debugging become extremely difficult. This is why I believe AI should be integrated into classrooms, but at the appropriate time in a student's education.

Educational systems may also need to adjust assignments and exams to reflect the growing presence of AI. At a certain point, students should be expected to know how to use AI responsibly as a tool. This could help prepare future generations for careers in industries where AI will likely become a normal part of everyday work, just as earlier generations benefited from increased access to computers and other forms of technology.
